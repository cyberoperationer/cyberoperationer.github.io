Experiment Overview

Between September and December 2024, 20 000 UK Government civil servants participated in two parallel AI pilots:

  • Mapping the Potential (Alan Turing Institute, June 2025) assessed generative-AI exposure across 80 public-sector activities using ONS time-use survey data :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}.
  • Microsoft 365 Copilot Trial (Government Digital Service, Sept–Dec 2024) captured real-world AI interactions and user feedback across 15 civil-service professions :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}.

Time-Savings Analysis

Both studies identify four routine activities that AI can fully automate without human oversight:

  • Organising meetings and related administration
  • Creating or updating appointments/bookings
  • Completing or processing standard forms
  • Creating or updating records, databases, or case files

In the Copilot trial, participants reported:

  • 26 minutes/day saved on document drafting
  • 19 minutes/day saved on presentation preparation
  • 13 working days saved per person per year (≈ 1 000 work-years across 20 000 participants) .

Monetary Conversion

  1. Median salary per civil servant (2024): £33 980
  2. Daily cost: £33 980 ÷ 260 ≈ £130.69
  3. Per-person saving: 13 days × £130.69 ≈ £1 699
  4. Cohort saving: 20 000 × £1 699 ≈ £33.98 million

Policy Implications

  1. Budgetary Reinvestment
    Redirect the £34 million annual efficiency dividend toward high-impact areas (e.g., digital infrastructure upgrades, frontline service enhancements), rather than reducing headcount.

  2. Reskilling & Role Redeployment

    • Develop AI-augmented career pathways: equip civil servants with data-analysis, policy-writing, and digital-service design skills.
    • Embed 20 hours/year of AI training into CPD curricula to standardize competency across grades and departments.

  3. Service Delivery Improvements

    • Freeing 13 working days per person can shorten application-processing backlogs by an estimated 10 %, improving citizen satisfaction and reducing wait times.
    • Introduce performance metrics that track both speed (e.g., case turnaround) and quality (e.g., error rates, customer feedback).

Conclusion

These findings demonstrate that strategic integration of generative AI can deliver measurable productivity gains—freeing resources for higher-value public-service activities—provided implementation is accompanied by targeted training, robust governance, and transparent reinvestment of efficiency savings.
